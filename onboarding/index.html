Your role

What a Forward Deployed Engineer does

An FDE is the bridge between our product and the customer's reality. You sit with clients, understand their NetSuite and operational workflows, and stand up safe AI agent automations — adapting PromptSpeak governance to each environment.

On the front line with customers

Run discovery, map workflows, and translate business change requests into governed agent automations — the Enterprise Agile way.

Build & configure

Author PromptSpeak policies and symbol frames, wire up MCP tool governance, and ship integrations against NetSuite and client systems.

Govern & de-risk

Make sure every agent action is validated, drift-checked, and held for human approval where the stakes are high. Safety is the product.

Feed the loop

Bring field learnings back into the product — what works, what doesn't, where we improve. Retrospectives drive the roadmap.

The product you'll deploy

PromptSpeak — pre-execution governance for AI agents

AI agents call tools — file writes, API requests, shell commands — with nothing between intent and execution. PromptSpeak intercepts every MCP tool call, validates it against deterministic rules, and blocks or holds risky operations for human approval, in a fraction of a millisecond, before anything runs.

The 6-stage governance pipeline

Every request flows left to right. The circuit breaker runs first — halted agents are stopped before any validation.

CRITICAL → Block (SQL injection, hardcoded secrets) HIGH → Hold for approval Cleared → Execute
Symbol frames in 30 seconds. Governance is expressed as 2–12 symbol frames, mode first: [Mode][Domain][Constraint?][Action][Entity?]. Example — ⊕◊▶β = "strict · financial · execute · secondary-agent." You'll learn to author and validate these in week one.
Your Anthropic Academy track

Onboarding roadmap

Adapted from the ERP Access internship & consulting program: a mentored path from orientation to live customer deployment. Pace flexes to your progress — your mentor checks in throughout.

Phase 1Weeks 1–2

Orientation & foundations

  • Welcome session, team intros, and the ERP Access mission, culture & values
  • Policies: code of conduct, confidentiality, security protocols, communication channels
  • Accounts & access provisioned (see the First-Week Checklist below)
  • NetSuite basics: navigation, CRM, order management, financials, reporting
  • Anthropic Academy fundamentals: agents, MCP, tool use, and AI safety
Phase 2Weeks 3–6

PromptSpeak & governed agents

  • The 6-stage pipeline end to end; authoring and validating symbol frames
  • Hands-on with the 56 MCP governance tools (validate, security scan, hold/approve)
  • Build a sandbox automation against a NetSuite test instance
  • Shadow your mentor on a live Admin-as-a-Service engagement
Phase 3Weeks 7–10

Forward deployment

  • Run discovery with a real client: map workflows & change requests
  • Draft a specification doc and a governance policy for the engagement
  • Configure and deploy a governed agent with human-in-the-loop approvals
  • Own UAT → production sign-off with your mentor as backstop
Phase 4Weeks 11–12

Review & retrospective

  • Present your deployment to the team: what worked, what to improve
  • Feed learnings back into the PromptSpeak roadmap
  • Evaluation, feedback, and your ongoing FDE development plan
